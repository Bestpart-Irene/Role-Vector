\begin{abstract}
LLM agent personas are increasingly used as proxies for human roles in synthetic research, yet they
are typically validated only at the output level. We argue output plausibility is necessary but not
sufficient, and present a reusable \emph{construct-validation} pipeline that validates personas at the
activation level. We extract prompted role personas into activation-space vectors
(role-minus-default, score-2-plus weighting, balanced in/out-of-domain), and test whether they are
domain-sensitive, stable across repeated extraction, and separable from other roles. % {{one-line headline result}}
\end{abstract}

\section{Method}
\textbf{Roles.} Six reported roles plus a default-assistant subtraction baseline, including two close
contrasts (M\&A lawyer vs.\ corporations-law professor; general vs.\ corporations-law professor).
\textbf{Questions.} {{n\_questions}} mixed-domain questions across {{n\_families}} families; every role
answers every question ({{n\_in\_domain}} in-domain $+$ {{n\_ood}} out-of-domain).
\textbf{Judging.} A domain-aware $0$--$3$ role-adherence rubric (judge model: {{judge\_model}}).
\textbf{Construction.} Score-2-plus weighted, role-minus-default activations:
\[ v_{r,l,s} = \frac{\sum_i w_i h_i}{\sum_i w_i},\quad w=3\,(\text{score }3),\ w=2\,(\text{score }2),\ w=0\,(\text{else}); \quad
\hat v_{r,l,s} = v^{\text{in}}_{r,l,s} + v^{\text{ood}}_{r,l,s}. \]
